## Signals :

- What is a signal
	- A function of time or space that describes the evolution of some quantity or variable
	- Signals can be thought of as carrying information, or more broadly as representing measures of information, energy, or materials.
	- Signals can be characterized as discrete or continuous time, stochastic or deterministic, have infinite or finite duration, and more. 

## System :

- Something that operates on signals inputting a signal and outputting a signal
	- Ex: Compressing, sensing, transmitting, analyzing. 
- Example systems: filters, antenna arrays, microphones, digital cameras, neural networks, etc

## Methods for signals and systems :

- Tools used to describe model and understand signals and systems:
	- Signal characteristics:
		- Energy, bandwidth smoothness
	- System characteristics: 
		- linearity, time invariance, stability
	- Representations and transforms:
		- Time domain, z-transform, Laplace/Fourier transform
	- For linear time invariance (LTI) systems
		- Freq response, impulse response, convolution
