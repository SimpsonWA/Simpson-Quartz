A point $x^* \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is a **strict local minimizer** of $f$ if there exists a neighborhood $U(x^*)$ containing $x^*$ such that:  
$$
f(x^*) < f(x), \quad \forall x \in U(x^*), \quad x \neq x^*.
$$  
This means that $x^*$ is the **unique** minimum within its local neighborhood—no other point in $U(x^*)$ attains the same function value as $f(x^*)$.
