
A point $x^* \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is a **local minimizer** of $f$ if there exists a neighborhood $U(x^*)$ containing $x^*$ such that:  
$$
f(x^*) \leq f(x), \quad \forall x \in U(x^*).
$$  
This means that $x^*$ is the lowest function value within a small region around it, but not necessarily the global minimum of $f$.
