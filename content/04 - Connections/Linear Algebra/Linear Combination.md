**Idea**: A linear combination is essentially a weighted sum of vectors, formed by multiplying each vector in a collection by a scalar and then adding the results together.

**Linear Combination**: let $M = \{\vec{v_1},\vec{v_2},.....,\vec{v_m}\}$ be a collection of vectors in a vector space $S$. Then a linear combination of vectors $M$ is a sum of the form:
$$
	\sum_{i=1}^{n} a_i \vec{v_i} = a_1\vec{v_1}+a_2\vec{v_2}+...+a_n\vec{v_n}
$$
for some $a_1,a_2,...,a_n \in R$

This concept naturally leads to the idea of the [[Span]] of a set of vectors: the **span** of $M$, denoted $\text{Span}(M)$, is the set of **all** linear combinations of the vectors in $M$.
